<p><p>The vegetable extracts market is categorized into three primary types: form, fluids, and paste. Form extracts typically include dry powders made by dehydrating vegetables, preserving their nutrients and flavors. Fluid extracts consist of concentrated liquid forms, often used in cooking and food preparation for added taste and nutrition. Paste extracts are thick, concentrated mixtures that maintain the essence of the vegetable, making them ideal for sauces and spreads. Each type serves distinct culinary applications and consumer preferences in flavor enhancement.</p></p>
